1. Purpose of this Lab
In this lab, you will:
1. Form your project team.
2. Choose the mobile app you will develop for the final exam/project.
3. Write a detailed project proposal describing:
o The purpose of the app.
o Target users.
o Features and screens.
o Navigation and UX.
o Work distribution and timeline.

This proposal will be graded and will define what you are expected to deliver in the final project.

Be a mobile application relevant to the technologies used in this course.
2. Provide real user value.
3. Contain at least:
o 5–8 main screens.
o Persistent data storage (local or remote).
o At least one non-trivial feature, such as:
▪ Login/authentication (real or mock).
▪ CRUD functions (create, view, edit, delete).
▪ Search or filtering.
▪ Dynamic lists or detail views.

5. What Is Graded in This Lab (Proposal Marking Criteria)
The proposal grade is based on clarity, feasibility, and structure—not only code.
Proposed grading criteria:
1. Clarity and completeness of the idea – 25%
o Clear explanation of purpose and target users.
o Appropriate and realistic scope.
2. Functional requirements – 20%
o Well-defined, structured, and meaningful features.
3. Screens and navigation – 20%
o Logical flow.
o Clear understanding of all main screens.
4. Non-functional requirements – 15%
o Shows awareness of usability and performance considerations.
5. Work plan and distribution – 10%
o Realistic milestones.
o Clear responsibilities.
